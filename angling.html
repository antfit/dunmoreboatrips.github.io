Angling in Dunmore East

With the skipper's deep local knowledge, we can offer a tailored experience for every angler

Whether you are a novice or an experienced angler, young or old - Dunmore Boat Trips will ensure your trip is one to remember.

Dunmore Boat Trips offers angling charters on a daily or half daily basis. You can charter the boat for a private group or join another group. Maximum group size is twelve excluding the skipper. If you have more than twelve we would be happy to split up your group with half in the morning and half in the afternoon or multiple days and so on. We will work with you to meet the requirements of your group.

With our local knowledge, you will always be given the best possible chance to reel in a big catch.

Angling experiences we offer

We offer a range of angling experiences to suit all levels

Reef Bank Fishing

Dunmore Boat Trips offers Reef Bank Fishing about 15 to 20 minutes from Dunmore East Harbour. The ground there is good fishing ground for Pollock, Ling, Ballan Wrasse, Cuckoo Wrasse, Haddock, Cod, Conger Eel, Mackerel etc. Most of our fishing is done on the drift, over reefs with sandy ground between. Our inshore bottom fishing ranges from 8 metres to 45 metres.

Shark Fishing

The Blue Sharks fishing season is usually from mid June to October, with its peak period being between July and September when the water temperatures rise to about 13 / 14 degrees Celsius. Irish Blue Sharks range from 9kg in weight to over 45kg and although they are an oceanic species, they often come less than five miles from land.

Blue Shark Fishing is normally done on the drift. Apart from the traces and a float which we supply, blue shark fishing does not call for any special tackle.

Specimen Hunting

For our more extreme anglers, Dunmore Boat Trips offers specimen hunting. With our local knowledge, you will always be given the best possible chance to reel in a big catch. See our chart below to see the best times for catching the different species around the Dunmore East bay.

Bass Angling

Bass is often considered by many anglers to be one of the most desired species caught on rod and line. This powerful fish with its spiked dorsal fin and forked tail can be found very close to land and may be caught from beaches, estuaries, rocky headlands and piers. The best method we find is to use live sand eels on a long flowing trace or ragworm and sometimes peeler crab. Bass fishing is recommended for more experienced anglers.

Angling Competitions

The Keltoi Warrier is available for hire for Open Boat Competitions throughout Ireland. Early booking is advisable to guarantee your required dates.

Wreck Fishing

Local wrecks and kelp grounds provide plenty of sport with Cod, Pollock, Ling and Whiting always to be found. Not far away from Dunmore East lie numerous wrecks including the popular Manchester Engineer (sunk during WW1) and the Dutchman (1960s), both reachable within two hours of harbour.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the most common questions anglers ask us

What about safety equipment?

The safety of our passengers and crew is of utmost importance. The boat is equipped accordingly over and above the Department’s recommendations.

  • 16-man Life Raft
  • E.P.I.R.B.S.
  • Life Jackets
  • Lifebuoys
  • Flares
  • Comprehensive First Aid Kit
  • Fire Extinguishers
  • Fire Blankets

Do you provide bait?

We not normally provide bait to passengers. Mackerel (our most popular bait) is usually feathered on the way to the fishing grounds.

What happens to our catch?

We release most of our catch, with species such as Blue Shark and Tope tagged and released, so that the Central Fisheries Board can monitor them. You are free to take home other species such as Cod, Mackerel, Ling and Pollock.

What tackle should I bring?

You will usually need a 30lb class down tide outfit for general fishing i.e. Tope and Conger. You could also bring 12 - 20lb uptider / downtider for Pollack, Bass, Ray and so on. Braid fishes well in the stronger runs of tide and heads from 5oz to 1 1/2 oz should suffice.

What clothing should I bring?

We recommend you wear many layers of clothing rather than one heavy item. It is usually much cooler at sea due to the breeze being stronger. A good set of waterproofs is also advisable.

Do I need to bring food?

Copious amounts of tea and coffee are provided onboard FOC. It is recommended you bring a packed lunch, this can be included if necessary at extra cost. There is a food heating facility onboard.
